01Editor's Note

Structure and detail are the throughline in everything I write. Whether it's a culture profile, a tactical breakdown of a football match, or a script for a YouTube devlog, I start the same way: find the actual story inside the brief, cut what doesn't serve it, and get it in front of the right reader without wasting their time.

That range is deliberate, not scattered. I've worked across culture and lifestyle features, sports journalism, YouTube scriptwriting, SEO-driven content, and brand strategy — because editorial thinking travels. The instincts that make a profile piece land are the same ones that tighten a show rundown or make a piece of SEO copy easier to actually read. Editing other contributors' work and managing the workflow behind a publication has taught me as much about writing as writing itself has.

Going forward, I'm looking for editorial roles that put that range to use — positions that combine long-form storytelling with content and SEO strategy, where good writing and good discoverability aren't treated as separate jobs.
